# GLP-1 obesity market — draft

## 1. Market size and trajectory

The two incumbent suppliers already realize material obesity-segment revenue
[source: company filings], and analyst projections point to continued multi-year
growth [source: analyst market-size estimate]. The projection range is wide, so
any single point estimate should be treated as indicative rather than precise.

## 2. Supplier landscape

Supply is concentrated in two incumbents whose approved labels define the
current market [source: incumbent disclosures]. A set of late-stage entrants —
oral incretins and next-generation dual/triple agonists — is advancing
[source: pipeline trackers]; these could expand the addressable market while also
introducing price competition.

## 3. Demand drivers

Obesity prevalence in major markets is high and rising
[source: public-health prevalence data], which underpins durable structural
demand. Coverage and awareness are expanding but remain uneven across payers
[needs source], so realized demand will lag underlying prevalence.

## 4. Supply constraints

Injectable-pen fill-finish capacity has been the binding constraint on supply;
capacity expansions are underway [source: manufacturing commentary]. Until they
land, supply — not demand — is the near-term governor on revenue.

## 5. Downside risks

Two risks could compress the market: reimbursement tightening and step-therapy
requirements [needs source], and price compression as oral entrants reach the
market [source: pipeline trackers]. Neither is imminent, but both bear on the
durability question.

## 6. Implication for the decision

The bottom line: the market is large and structurally supported by rising
prevalence, supply-constrained in the near term, and exposed over a 3–5 year
horizon to access and competition risk. That argues for a sized theme position
with attention to the coverage trajectory as the key swing factor.
