# GLP-1 Obesity Market — Research Brief

## Executive summary

The GLP-1 obesity market is large, structurally supported by rising obesity
prevalence, and supply-constrained in the near term. Over a 3–5 year horizon its
durability turns mainly on payer coverage and on competition from oral and
next-generation entrants. The read for an investment committee: a sized thematic
position is warranted, with the coverage trajectory tracked as the key swing
factor. Two figures in this brief rest on estimates rather than confirmed
sources and are labelled as such.

## 1. Market size and trajectory

The two incumbent suppliers already realize material obesity-segment revenue,
and projections point to continued multi-year growth. The projection range is
wide, so treat any point estimate as indicative.

## 2. Supplier landscape

Supply is concentrated in two incumbents whose approved labels define today's
market. Late-stage entrants — oral incretins and next-generation dual/triple
agonists — are advancing and could both expand the market and introduce price
competition.

## 3. Demand drivers

High and rising obesity prevalence underpins durable structural demand. Coverage
and awareness are expanding but remain uneven across payers (estimate — not yet
sourced), so realized demand will lag underlying prevalence.

## 4. Supply constraints

Injectable-pen fill-finish capacity has been the binding constraint; expansions
are underway. Until they land, supply rather than demand governs near-term
revenue.

## 5. Downside risks

- **Reimbursement tightening** and step-therapy requirements could compress
  volume (estimate — not yet sourced).
- **Price compression** as oral entrants reach the market.

## 6. Implication for the decision

Large market, near-term supply governor, multi-year access-and-competition
exposure. Net: size the theme, and watch the coverage trajectory.
